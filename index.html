See what SalesHedge catches.

Here's what happens when a rep enters a deal that looks healthy on the surface.

What the rep enters
"Champion is excited, demo went great, they want to move forward. Close date is end of this month. Haven't met with the VP yet but my contact says she'll handle it internally."
SalesHedge detects 2 risk patterns
Risk: Champion without economic buyer access

"She'll handle it internally" is the single most common phrase preceding a deal stall. Your champion may be enthusiastic but lacks the authority or the internal credibility to drive a budget decision without executive involvement.

Recommended play

Ask your champion to arrange a 15-minute alignment call with the VP this week. Position it as "making sure we're building the right business case for her priorities," not as a qualification test. Offer to co-present.

Risk: Close date lacks supporting evidence

The rep says "end of this month" but no mutual action plan exists, no procurement timeline has been confirmed, and no business case has been presented. This is an aspirational date, not a committed one.

Recommended play

Before your next conversation, ask: "What specifically needs to happen between now and the end of the month for this to close?" If the buyer can't enumerate the steps, the date is not real.
